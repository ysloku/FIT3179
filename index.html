Economic Analysis of the Human Development Index

The Human Development Index (HDI) was created to emphasize that people and their capabilities should be the ultimate criteria for measuring the development of a country, not economic growth alone. The HDI can also be used to question national policy choices, asking how two countries with the same level of GNI per capita can end up with different human development outcomes.The Human Development Index (HDI) is a summary measure of average achievement in key dimensions of human development: a long and healthy life, being knowledgeable and have a decent standard of living.

The Components of HDI - 1) Health

The health dimension is assessed by life expectancy at birth

In each country, when normalized this component is equal to 0 when life expectancy is 20 and equal to 1 when life expectancy is 85, Hong Kong with highest life expectancy such as show in this chart .

2) Standard of Living

The standard of living dimension is used for measuring the gross national income per capita. The HDI uses the income, to showcase the importance of income with increasing GNI.

Countries ranked from 1 to 66 such as Norway are considered to be of very high HDI, ranked 67 to 119 are considered to be of high HDI, ranked 120 to 156 are considered to be of medium HDI such as United States, and ranked 157 to 189 are considered to be of low HDI such as China.

3) Education

The education dimension is used for measuring mean of years of schooling for adults aged 25 years and more and expected years of schooling for children of school entering age

It shows that an individual's level of education is significantly related to having a liberalized mindset in higher HDI compared to lower HDI countries. Moreover, through this increased liberalization, education resulted in higher innovation ratings in higher HDI countries.

Takeaway or the importance of the HDI

It is important to consider the social measures of development when finding the entire development of a nation. Human Development Index is used for measuring the socio economic factors and is very effective in measuring the performance of a country in terms of these factors. HDI is used as a tool in understanding the socio-economic status of nations globally every year, this proves to be a reliable indicator of the development of the nations.
